FHA vs Conventional — what every first-time buyer should know

FHA Loans
  • 3.5% minimum down (credit 580+), 10% if 500–579
  • 1.75% upfront MIP rolled into your loan balance
  • Annual MIP: 0.55% for most 30yr loans
  • MIP lasts the life of the loan if down < 10%
  • More lenient debt-to-income ratios
  • Down payment gifts from family allowed
  • FHA loan limits vary by county (~$524k most FL counties)
Conventional Loans
  • 3% minimum (some programs) — typically 5%+
  • No upfront MIP — lower loan amount from day one
  • PMI: typically 0.5%–1.2% based on credit & down
  • PMI automatically cancels at 80% LTV
  • Usually requires 620+ credit score
  • Lower rates with good credit vs FHA
  • No loan limits (conforming)
The smart strategy for most FHA borrowers: FHA gets you in the door, but plan to refinance to conventional once you hit 20% equity. That single move permanently eliminates MIP — often saving $100–$250/month for the rest of the loan. Making biweekly payments accelerates your path to that 20% milestone.
